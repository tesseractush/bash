Parallelization

This module covers running commands in parallel and managing background jobs in Bash.

Background jobs (&)

Description: Run a command in the background by appending &.

Syntax: command &

Example: sleep 10 &

Tip: Use for long-running tasks you don't want to block the terminal.

wait

Description: Waits for background jobs to finish.

Syntax: wait [job]

Example: sleep 5 & pid=$!; wait $pid; echo "Done"

Tip: Use wait with $! to wait for a specific job.

jobs

Description: Lists background jobs in the current shell.

Syntax: jobs

Example: sleep 10 &; jobs

Tip: Use fg to bring a job to the foreground.
